About the Initiative

Addressing the complexities of urban traffic networks through proactive digital twin engineering.

The Problem

Bengaluru is one of the most rapidly expanding urban centers in the world. With growth comes a dense network of commuters, commercial vehicles, and infrastructure nodes. Disruptions to this network happen daily due to variety of triggers:

Incidents & Construction

Vehicle breakdowns, minor accidents, metro rail expansion, and waterlogging immediately paralyze local streets.

Major Gatherings

VIP movements, political rallies, and massive sporting events create immediate bottlenecks on major corridors.

The Challenge

Traditional municipal command centers operate reactively. When an accident or waterlogging occurs, traffic signals are adjusted, or patrols are dispatched after gridlocks have already formed. By then, congestion has already rippled across neighboring sectors, causing hours of delay.

The Solution

Traffic Twin Bengaluru shifts traffic management from reactive recovery to proactive prevention. By establishing a virtual representation of the road network, our platform runs real-time simulations to predict the impact of disruptions before they occur.

When an incident report is received, operators run a simulation to see exactly where traffic will spill over in the next 15, 30, and 60 minutes. This allows the system to recommend optimal diversion paths and update surrounding signal timings before the bottleneck manifests.

High-Fidelity Digital Forecasting

Our platform evaluates multi-strategy detours, measures global city impact, and provides actionable, police-ready coordination recommendations automatically.

Underlying Technology Stack

Combining modern deep learning models with classic graph routing algorithms to build a reliable city-state engine.

Spatio-Temporal GNNs

Utilizes PyTorch-based Graph Neural Networks to forecast congestion propagation dynamically across the topological network structure over time.

Machine Learning

Uses LightGBM and XGBoost predictors trained on past incident databases to evaluate duration, closure risk, and overall severity levels.

Digital Twin Graph

Models Bengaluru's primary, secondary, and tertiary arterials via NetworkX MultiDiGraphs directly initialized from OpenStreetMap data.

Adaptive Optimization

Computes real-time junction pressure and coordination offsets to implement Surtrac-style adaptive green wave signal timing.
